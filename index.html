Greetings, everyone. My name is Diana Phan and I am from San Gabriel, California but I have lived in and raised in Silicon Valley for a majority of my life. Currently, I am a Junior year student at the University of Riverside, California. My current goal is to become a children's psychologist or medical social worker. A huge passion of mine is to help those who are around me in any way that I can. That is why I chose to double major in two subjects. As a sociology major, one of my biggest focuses is to learn about how the world socializes with one another and connects. This is so that I can dig deeper into tackling some underlying social issues and concepts that our world exhibits today. Furthermore, as a psychology major, I am interested in how a person deals with mental illnesses and everyday psychological problems. My interest here is to understand more about the inner being of an individual and find ways how they can relieve their problems.

I have also involved myself in quite a few activities and organizations during my time at community college and university. Many of the associations that I am currently in are ethnic and culture based, since I am a very social person who sees the importance of cultural value. For instance, I am a member of the Vietnamese Student Association, the Chinese Student Association, and Hallyu (a club that introduces and teaches students about Korean culture, traditions, and media). In addition, I love interacting in community service organizations because it helps me feel close to the environment and its people. Some of the organizations that I am apart of as of right now are Circle K and Calprig. These clubs incorporate lots of volunteer work which promote fellowship, leadership, and service.

I have learned a lot of skills while experiencing the life of being a college student. Some of my skill sets include time-management, leadership, communication, problem-solving, photography, social analysis and insisting on mental illness. Additionally, I am well experienced with teaching and leading children and teenagers. This is due to my volunteer experience at children's hospitals and high schools forums. Furthermore, I am also extremely knowledgeable about art history and photography of all kinds due to my experience as a photographer. I am also very interested and greatly informed in ideas such as asian american dynamics and feminism.
